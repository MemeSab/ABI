Before jumping into schedules, it's vital to assess your current relationship with movement. Take a few quiet minutes to answer these questions honestly. There are no wrong answers.

1. What feels realistic today?

(Considering your energy levels, work commitments, and family demands right now...)

2. What would make me feel good afterwards?

(Focus on the mental shift, joint relief, or breathing space rather than calorie burning...)

3. What's one small step I can take?

(A step so small it requires virtually zero willpower to execute...)

Daily Movement Ideas

Every day is different. Some days you will have abundant energy, and other days you will need restorative comfort. Align your activity to how you feel.

Gentle Days (Low Energy / Restorative)
  • 5-minute morning stretch
  • Slow, mindful neighborhood walk
  • Gentle mobility exercises
  • Restorative yoga poses
  • Shoulder rolls at your desk
  • Deep breathing & neck stretches
Moderate Days (Moderate Energy / Steady)
  • Brisk, 20-minute power walk
  • Kitchen dance to your favorite song
  • Steady pilates sequence
  • Active gardening or yard work
  • Leisurely bike ride
  • Light water aerobics or swim
Strong Days (High Energy / Empowering)
  • Strength/resistance workout
  • Trail run or jogging
  • Dynamic bodyweight circuit
  • Uphill hike in nature
  • High-intensity interval session
  • Lap swimming or spin class

The Five-Minute Rule

5

Promise yourself just five minutes. If you're feeling unmotivated, make a contract with yourself to start for just five minutes. Set a timer.

If you continue, wonderful. More often than not, the friction is simply in starting. Once you begin moving, your physiology shifts and motivation follows.

If you don't, you've still honored the promise. If the timer rings and you genuinely want to stop, do so. You kept your word, reinforcing self-trust.

Consistency matters more than perfection.

Abi
Abi's Tip "It is better to walk for five minutes daily than to run for an hour once a month. Rhythms beat volume."

Abigail Stocks Mindset Shift

Mindset Matters

How we speak to ourselves about movement shapes our habits. Replacing rules-based, restrictive terminology with gentle, empowering choices removes the weight of expectation.

Instead of

"I have to exercise."

Try

"I get to move my body."

Instead of

"I need to do an hour for it to count."

Try

"Even ten minutes makes a difference."

Instead of

"I ruined my streak, I might as well give up."

Try

"I can start fresh with my next choice."

Instead of

"It has to be intense and sweat-heavy."

Try

"Gentle movement is highly valuable."

Movement & Mindset Guide 08
Abigail Stocks Habit Architecture

Build Healthy Habits

We build lasting routines by tying new activities to established behaviors. This is called Habit Stacking, and it uses your brain's existing neurological pathways to make action automatic.

1. Existing Habit "After I boil the morning kettle..."
2. Add Small Movement "...I will do 5 gentle calf raises."
3. Repeat Daily Build consistency without willpower
4. Permanent Habit Movement becomes automatic self-care
Abi
Abi's Tip "Stack your new habits onto things you already do automatically. This reduces the friction of starting."
